Creating Your Account

The Cull Diary requires a free account to sync your records securely across devices. Open the diary from the main First Light app by tapping Cull Diary in the header.

After creating your account, check your email for a confirmation link. Once confirmed, sign in with your email and password.

If you forget your password, tap Forgot password? on the sign-in screen. Enter the email for your account and use the link in the reset email to choose a new password (check spam if nothing arrives).

Tip: You only need to sign in once. The app keeps you signed in between sessions automatically.

6

Editing & Deleting

From the detail view, tap Edit to reopen the full form with all fields pre-filled. Make your changes and save. The entry updates in place — no duplicate created.

Tap Delete to permanently remove an entry. You'll be asked to confirm. Deleted entries cannot be recovered.

Warning: Deletion is permanent. If you need a record for compliance, export the entry as PDF before deleting.
7

Season & Ground Management

The diary organises entries by stalking season (August–July). Use the season dropdown in the diary header to switch between seasons.

Grounds (permissions / estates) let you tag entries by where you stalk. To add or review saved grounds:

1. Open Stats → on the Season Plan card tap Set targets or Edit (wording depends on whether you already have targets).
2. Open the By ground tab and use Add ground to name a permission.
3. Saved grounds then appear as tappable options in the entry form’s Permission / Ground field.

Tip: Grounds persist across seasons. You can remove a ground from the targets sheet when you no longer need it in the list.
8

Cull Targets & Season Plan

Set cull targets to compare your plan to actual culls. From Stats, use Set targets or Edit on the Season Plan card (same sheet — the label depends on whether anything is set yet).

Season total

Steppers for each species (male / female) set your headline season plan. A small footer under the grid shows total ♂, ♀, and animals. The sheet opens here first.

By ground

After you save at least one named ground, use this tab to split targets by permission. Unassigned is an optional row for animals you still count in the season but do not park on a named ground — the app explains how it lines up with the headline when you save.

While the sheet is open, Season total and By ground stay aligned in the background (no need to flip tabs just to preview numbers). If you close with changes you have not saved, you will be asked to confirm so nothing is dropped by mistake.

If the current season is still empty but last season had targets, you may see Copy from previous season on the sheet.

Plan chips on Stats

When grounds or ground targets exist, chips appear above the plan — e.g. Overview plus each ground (and Unassigned where it applies). Overview can follow the sum of ground targets when that is how you are running the season; tapping a ground filters the plan and stats to that place.

Stats & Charts

The Stats tab shows season totals, the cull map, your plan card, and deeper breakdowns. The season selector on Stats matches your diary season.

Cull map

Every entry with a pin appears on the interactive map. Use Map / Satellite to change the base layer. Under the map, the species chips control which pins are shown and they rebuild all stats and charts below for that species (or All). Those chips are separate from the Diary tab species filter, which only narrows the entry list — choose the same species under the map if you want stats to match what you were viewing in the list.

Charts & breakdowns

Below the plan / exports area, open Charts & breakdowns to reveal sections such as:

• Monthly cull — activity through the season
• Cull by ground — when you use grounds
• Species and sex splits
• Age class, shooter, carcass destination
• Calibre and shot distance (when those fields are filled on entries)

Each block respects your current season on Stats and the map species chip (see above). Empty sections show a short “no data” message.

Always visible on Stats

• Headline counters (animals, kg, etc.) — follow the map species chip once you change it from All
• Season Plan — targets vs actuals; the Overview / ground chips above the plan choose which permission’s entries count as “actual” (separate from the map species filter)
• Syndicates card — shared team targets when you belong to a syndicate (see step 13)
• Exports — personal CSV / PDF / Summary (step 10)

Exporting Data

You can export your records in three ways:

Export CSV — all entries as spreadsheet
Export PDF — formatted list
Season Summary PDF — full report with stats

CSV — opens in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app. Includes all fields.
PDF list — simple formatted list of all entries with key details.
Season Summary — a polished report with header stats, species breakdown, cull plan progress, and a full entry table. Ideal for sending to estates or DMGs.

You can also export a single entry as PDF from its detail view — useful for individual cull records.

Summary PDF can be narrowed with the summary filter modal (season and ground) when you need a report for an estate or your own files.

Tip: Use the season and ground filter dropdowns in the summary modal to export a specific subset of your data.
11

Offline Mode

The diary works without signal. When you're offline:

• New entries are saved to a local queue on your device
• A banner shows how many entries are queued and storage used
• When signal returns, entries sync automatically
• If storage runs low, photos are stripped to save space (you'll be notified on sync)

Note: Photos are large. If you're offline for extended periods, the app may need to strip photos from queued entries to stay within browser storage limits. Entries themselves are always preserved.
12

Account & Privacy

Your diary data is stored securely in Supabase with row-level security — only you can access your entries. No one else, including the developer, can see your data.

From the Stats tab, scroll to the bottom to find:

• Sign out — signs you out on this device
• Delete account — permanently removes your account and all diary data

For full details, read the Privacy Policy.

13

Syndicates & Team Exports

Syndicates let a small team share group cull targets alongside personal diaries. Access is invite-only — you join when a manager sends you an invite, or you create a syndicate and invite others from the flow in the app.

On Stats, the Syndicates card shows each team you belong to with the same style of plan bars as your personal Season Plan. Managers can create syndicates and manage invites from there. If you manage a syndicate and want to leave, promote another active member to manager first.

If you are a manager, an extra block — Syndicate exports — can appear under your personal exports. It offers CSV, PDF, and Summary for team-wide diary data (everyone in the syndicate), not just your own entries. Members who are not managers do not see that block.

Invite links are short-lived and usage-limited by default (for safer sharing), and active invites can be copied or revoked from the manage sheet.

When a syndicate is tied to a named permission (ground filter), team counts only include your culls if Permission / Ground on the entry matches that name exactly (same spelling). When you create a syndicate, you can pick any name already in your ground list (including labels synced from teams you belong to) or Other / new ground… — that choice sets the new team’s filter only; it does not change other syndicates. The app keeps your ground list aligned with each syndicate’s current filter when you sign in, open Stats (syndicate card loads), or open Add entry / Edit entry — so if a manager renames the syndicate ground, the new name is added to your list on next sync (old entries still keep their old label until you edit them). On Add / Edit entry, you can now set Syndicate attribution per cull (or leave personal-only), so one cull counts toward one syndicate at most. If a ground label matches exactly one active syndicate filter, attribution auto-selects that syndicate for convenience. The ground dropdown still uses Syndicate permissions / My permissions groups so you can see which labels match a syndicate filter. If you type a different name or use “Other / new ground…” on an entry without matching the team filter, those culls may not count toward the team.

On Add / Edit entry, the ground dropdown uses Syndicate permissions and My permissions groups so you can see which labels are tied to syndicate filters (a name can appear under syndicate if it matches any syndicate you belong to).

Tip: Syndicate features need a live connection and a signed-in account; they are optional — your personal diary works the same without them.

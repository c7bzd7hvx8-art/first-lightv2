Key Legislation
Deer Act 1991England & Wales. Seasons, legal hours, vehicle/lamp offences, sale of venison.
Deer (Scotland) Act 1996Scotland. Similar provisions + NatureScot powers to order deer control.
Wild Mammals (Protection) Act 1996UK-wide. Prohibition of intentional cruelty to wild mammals, including injured deer.
Animal Welfare Act 2006England & Wales. Duty to take reasonable steps to end unnecessary suffering.
Food Hygiene Regulations 2006Requires carcass chilling to ≤7°C. Applies to venison entering food chain.
DSC1 — Exam Structure
Written paper50 MCQ — 80% pass mark (40/50)
Wild Game Meat Hygiene40 MCQ — 80% pass mark (32/40)
Visual ID20 slides — species & sex (16/20 to pass)
Safety walkSimulated stalk with safety scenarios (10/10)
Shooting assessment2 shots at 100m prone · 2 at 70m off sticks · 2 at 10–20m humane dispatch
DSC2Portfolio — witnessed stalk with DMQ Approved Witness. Must complete within 3 years of registration.
DSC2 grants Large Game Trained Hunter (LGTH) status — required to supply venison to an Approved Game Handling Establishment (AGHE).
Gralloch & Carcass Obligations
Chill temp≤7°C (Food Hygiene Regs). Target ≤4°C.
PPE requiredGloves always — zoonotic risk (leptospirosis, E. coli)
Notifiable findingbTB, CWD, FMD, anthrax — must report to APHA immediately
Condemn carcass ifTB lesions, extensive pneumonia, anthrax, severe peritonitis
Declaration requiredLGTH declaration needed when supplying venison to game dealer

Notifiable Diseases — Must Report to APHA
DiseaseSigns
bTBEnlarged caseous lymph nodes (jaw, shoulder), lung lesions
CWDWeight loss, lost fear of humans, salivation, abnormal gait — FATAL
FMDLameness, blisters on feet and mouth, excessive salivation
BluetongueFacial swelling, nasal discharge, blue tongue, lameness
AnthraxSudden death, massively enlarged black spleen — DO NOT HANDLE
Suspicion alone is sufficient and legally obligatory to report. Do not wait for confirmation.
Common Non-Notifiable Conditions
Liver flukePale fluke tracks / damage in liver. Needs mud snail habitat.
LungwormWorms in airways. Heavy burden = poor condition, condemn lungs.
Sarcoptic mangeHair loss, thickened skin, intense scratching.
Deer kedsFlat wingless flies in coat. Can bite humans.
LeptospirosisZoonotic — wear gloves. Carried in kidneys/urine.
ElaphostrongylusMuscle worm — affects muscles and nervous system in Red/Sika.
Gralloch Inspection Checklist
Lymph nodesShould be small and pale. Enlarged / caseous = possible bTB
LungsPink-red, spongy. Grey lesions or miliary spots = TB
LiverDeep red-brown, firm. White spots or fluke = condemn liver
SpleenNormal = small red organ. Massively enlarged, black = anthrax — do not handle
GutPeritonitis, unusual odour, widespread lesions = condemn carcass

If You Wound a Deer
1. ReloadImmediately — then wait 20–30 min (heart/lung) or longer (gut)
2. MarkMark firing point and strike site before approaching
3. ReadBright red frothy = lung (20 min). Dark = heart/liver (30 min). Green/foul = gut (4+ hrs)
4. StopIf uncertain — stop searching. Call UKDTR. Random searching pushes animal further.
Legal dutyYou must make every reasonable effort to find and dispatch a wounded deer (Deer Act 1991)
Zoonotic Risks — Protect Yourself
LeptospirosisAlways wear gloves — contact with urine/kidneys
Lyme diseaseCheck for ticks after every outing. Remove promptly with tick tool.
E. coli / SalmonellaGloves + hygiene at gralloch and in larder
bTBAvoid inhaling dust near TB-suspect carcass. Report to APHA.
